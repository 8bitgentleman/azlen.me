Three engagement factors

Why?

  • Because none of these engagement factors works well on its own. A goal without knowing the tools or space (the how or what) to achieve that goal is simply just a dream. If you only have one engagement factor it can feel limiting or boring.

Most mediums we interact with are passive: books, movies, social media. However Games are inherently engaging mediums because they combine three important factors for engagement: goals, spaces, and tools.

My theory is that at least two of these engagement factors are required to create engagement

Most games provide all three engagement factors simultaneously, but in the case of books and movies, Static media often provide only one engagement factor at a time. At least two are required to create engagement. If you read a book that gives writing advice (tools), but you do not have a goal in mind to write something you may not internalize the advice well, or only later engage with it. However if you have a project that you're working on (a goal) then a book related to this project will give you either the space or the tools to reach towards your goals (Personal projects structure learning and engagement).

How to make writing engaging?

It might not be sufficient to simply create two engagement factors, there are many more factors at play such as the depth and complexity of those factors, how well they work together in tandem, and how "fun" the overall system is.

Thinking back to Three engagement factors, a common pattern across the examples is the environment of curiosity: enabling environments provide a space and tools to navigate that space towards your own personal goals (and not the goals that the environment places upon you like in games or school)
